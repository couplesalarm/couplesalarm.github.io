How it works

Built around one honest condition.

This only works if the person who needs to wake up can hear a sound their partner cannot. Couples Alarm checks that fit before either person is asked to rely on it.

  1. 01

    Choose who wakes first

    Set the roles for this schedule. The app preserves that request instead of silently swapping partners.

  2. 02

    Listen separately

    Each partner completes two short listening trials using only the built-in iPhone speaker.

  3. 03

    Preview together

    If a suitable bundled tone is found, both partners preview it and choose its sound style.

  4. 04

    Confirm at the bedside

    Run a real 10-second AlarmKit check from the intended bedside position before setup completes.

Designed not to guess

If the fit is not there, the app stops.

People, rooms, phones, and volume settings vary. If Couples Alarm cannot find a suitable bundled tone for the chosen schedule, it tells you to keep using an alarm you already trust.

Couples Alarm is an alarm utility. Its guided listening process is not a hearing test or medical assessment, and it does not guarantee wakefulness or that a partner will remain undisturbed.

Technical notes

Local, deterministic, and deliberately bounded.

The implementation is inspectable enough to discuss without turning the product into a medical claim.

72 bundled tones

Four sound styles across 18 carrier frequencies from 8.5–17 kHz. Tone files are generated and packaged ahead of time.

Deterministic selection

Personal listening responses are evaluated locally against explicit selection rules—no model, cloud scoring, or silent role reversal.

Native AlarmKit delivery

One-time and weekly alarms are scheduled through Apple’s AlarmKit on supported iPhones.

Private beta

Testing thoughtfully before launch.

Couples Alarm is not publicly downloadable from the App Store yet. Brian sends TestFlight invitations directly; there is no public sign-up or access-request form.

Setup and the real fit test are free. After setup, one calendar month of full access begins; continued full access is a $4.99 one-time purchase, not a subscription.
